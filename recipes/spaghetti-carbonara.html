Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish from Rome. This creamy, rich pasta is made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper. It's simple yet elegant, and when made properly, creates a silky sauce that coats every strand of pasta perfectly.

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 200g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 4 large eggs
  • 100g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • 50g Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt for pasta water
  • 2 cloves garlic (optional)

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil and cook spaghetti according to package directions until al dente.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, both cheeses, and a generous amount of black pepper.
  3. In a large skillet, cook pancetta over medium heat until crispy, about 8-10 minutes.
  4. If using garlic, add it to the pancetta for the last minute of cooking.
  5. Reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water before draining the spaghetti.
  6. Add the hot pasta to the skillet with pancetta and toss to combine.
  7. Remove from heat and quickly pour in the egg mixture while tossing continuously.
  8. Add pasta water gradually while tossing until you achieve a creamy consistency.
  9. Serve immediately with extra cheese and black pepper.
